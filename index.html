What is Bitcoin Cash?Bitcoin Cash (BCH) ExplainedBCH BasicsBCH FAQBitcoinCash

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) is a financial system without barriers, enabling easy, quick, and low-cost transactions anytime, anywhere.

It’s not just a currency; BCH is a movement for change that fosters innovation and connects users globally.

BCH is designed for everyone. It empowers individuals, whether a farmer or a trader, to participate in the global economy in new ways. BCH is about creating connections, not just about money. It speaks every language and belongs to everyone.

BCH is programmable money; handling Payments (Medium of Exchangemeans of exchange; MoE), Savings (Store of Value)SoV, Financial contractsDecentralized Finance (DeFi) & tokensmeme coin, memecoin, and much more.

Bitcoin Cash is truly scalable. As the number of BCH users grows, quick transactions and low fees will remain the gold standard.

BCH’s decentralized network of developers, nodes, and miners ensures no single point of failure.

CashFusion makes privacy easy, keeping you safe while maintaining all the non-custodial and low cost benefits of BCH.

Moria (MUSD)

Moria is an open-source protocol for secure, oracle-based borrowing against BCH collateral with 100% transparency of Proof-of-Reserves on the Bitcoin Cash network. It can be used to borrow tokens representing real world assets like USD, gold, sivler, or TradFi stocks. The first asset using Moria is MUSD, a USD-pegged stablecoin. It operates within a DeFi ecosystem, enabling transactions, lending, and trading without relying on centralized control.

Visit

StableHedge

StableHedge is a novel DeFi protocol built on the Bitcoin Cash (BCH) blockchain, designed to mitigate price volatility risks while enabling instant liquidity. By tokenizing AnyHedge contracts and integrating strategic hedging, StableHedge offers a stable, redeemable token pegged to USD value, eliminating lock-in periods and reducing counterparty fees.

But what about Bitcoin (BTC)?

In the early days, BTC was capable of instant & cheap transactions, however, today it is slow and expensive — during times of congestion, transactions can cost upwards of $100 and take hours, days, or even weeks to process. Third parties, custodians, and "second-layers" are needed to transact effectively, recreating many of the issues Bitcoin was designed to solve.

Today it is hailed as "digital gold"; not suitable for payments and not meant for everyone, a stark contrast from the white paper: "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System."

This didn't happen out of nowhere. Over the course of a multi-year endeavor, an obscure group of developers and economic actors gained control of the code base, stagnating development and artificially limiting this revolutionary technology.

Roger Ver and Steve Patterson documented the history of events leading to and following BTC's fall in Hijacking Bitcoin.
